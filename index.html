Professional Summary

Nobel Prize-winning theoretical physicist best known for developing the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics. My work has profoundly influenced the philosophy of science and our understanding of space, time, and gravity. Passionate about unifying the laws of physics and a dedicated advocate for civil rights and education.

Experience

1914 – 1933
Professor of Theoretical Physics
Humboldt University of Berlin & Kaiser Wilhelm Institute
Developed general relativity, predicted gravitational waves, and contributed to quantum statistics. Supervised numerous PhD candidates and lectured across Europe.
1912 – 1914
Professor of Theoretical Physics
ETH Zurich
Continued work on general relativity and gravitation. Introduced the concept of spacetime curvature.
1909 – 1911
Associate Professor
University of Zurich
Focused on statistical mechanics and quantum theory. Published influential papers on specific heat and light quanta.
1902 – 1909
Technical Expert (Patent Clerk)
Swiss Patent Office, Bern
Evaluated electromagnetic device patents. During this period wrote the “Annus Mirabilis” papers on the photoelectric effect, Brownian motion, special relativity, and mass–energy equivalence.
